Academic Research

I attended the University of Wisconsin, graduating in 2004 with a B.S. in Physics, Mathematics, and Astronomy. I earned a Ph.D. from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 2010 with a thesis on measuring galaxy cluster substructure with gravitational lensing flexion. Since then I have worked at the University of California Davis as a Postdoctoral Scholar (in addition to being a Lecturer - see below).

My research interests focus around the substructure of dark matter haloes. I currently work primarily with deep Hubble Space Telescope images of galaxy clusters using public data sets from programs such as CLASH and the Hubble Frontier Fields (I am a member of the Bradac team in the latter). You can read about my published results here and here.

Teaching

I am a passionate educator with a strong background in both physics and astronomy. While at UC Davis, I have been the instructor of record for these courses:

  • Physics 1A: A one quarter lecture-based classical mechanics course aimed at students in agriculture science majors.
  • Physics 7A,B,&C: A three quarter laboratory and active-learning focused series covering introductory physics and aimed at students in biomedical science majors.
  • Physics 9A: A one quarter lecture and lab classical mechanics course aimed at students in engineering and physics majors.

I have also, from 2013-2016, been an instructor for the UC Davis COSMOS summer program. Each summer we would lead 20-25 advanced high school students in a month-long immersive astrophysics program. This included courses and independent research projects, field trips, and rooftop observing with small telescopes. In 2016 I was the lead instructor for our astrophysics cluster and oversaw the expansion of our use of professional astronomy software in the program and an increased emphasis on teaching the students programming skills in Python.

Union Leadership

Since April 2011 I have been proud to be an elected member of the statewide Executive Board of the UC postdoc union - UAW 5810. I served 4 years as the chief Trustee on the board and then became the local unionFinancial Secretary. In addition to my specific roles overseeing the union's finances, as a leader and activist member I have worked to forward the goals of our members. I have been involved in political advocacy on the state and federal levels, worked to build the postdoc community on the UC Davis campus and statewide through union member engagement, and represented postdocs in grievances. As a member of the Executive Board, in 2015 and 2016, I was a member of the contract bargaining team for the union. Through these two negotiations we successfully negotiated two contracts that include groundbreaking expansions of postdoc rights and benefits with no concessions.
