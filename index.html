Benjamin Cain, PhD: Astrophysics Researcher, Educator, Union Leader

I study gravitational lensing by galaxy clusters. In particular, I measure the amount that cluster lenses bend background galaxies into arcs as a way to measure the small scale variations in the distribution of both baryonic and dark matter. This bending is called flexion, and is a quantitative measure of gradients in the local lensing convergence and shear fields. Read about my work on flexion HERE and HERE
